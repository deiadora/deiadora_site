Coherence architecture
for organisations, AI systems,
and the spaces between.

Most organisations do not have an incoherence problem. They have a coherence debt — accumulated over years of strategy that outpaced structure, values that were stated but never enacted, and systems built to perform alignment rather than produce it. This practice exists to locate, measure, and resolve that debt.

coherence defined

What coherence is

The condition in which a system's values, structure, language, and behaviour generate rather than deplete the energy required to sustain themselves.

A coherent system does not need to perform its identity. It is its identity — at every touchpoint, for every user, across every channel.

What coherence is not

Consistency of branding.

Compliance with stated values.

Strategic alignment as a slide deck.

Polished onboarding over broken trust.

Where incoherence lives

Between a stated value and the navigation that contradicts it.

Between an accessibility claim and a developer-first document structure.

Between a decentralisation promise and a single-authority voice in the docs.

Between what an AI system was trained to say and what the organisation actually does.

the intelligence layer

The playbook is the map.
The architecture is the territory it was drawn from.

This practice is grounded in a proprietary coherence architecture developed over 13 years of field research across human systems, quantum field theory, organisational design, and AI development. The architecture operates as an intelligence layer — not a framework to be handed to clients, but a diagnostic and generative model that produces outputs calibrated to each organisation's specific coherence field.

What clients receive is a 10-branch playbook: a structured, non-sequential set of interventions that can be delegated across functions, completed in any order, and implemented at the pace the organisation can sustain.

The 264 quantum keys are the engine. The 880 relational protocols govern how the system interacts across environments. Together they form what may be the first structured cognitive framework designed to operate coherently at human, team, organisational, and machine scale. AI models need a relational layer to work properly and safely, no matter the system. This is that layer.

how engagement works

01

Coherence audit

A full diagnostic across stated values, content, navigation, tone, and system architecture. Identifies gaps by severity: aligned, partial, misaligned, or critical.

02

Gap report + playbook

A structured 10-branch intervention plan. Non-sequential. Delegable across teams. Each branch names what to remove, replace, or rebuild — and why.

03

Agent deployment

Custom AI agents built on the coherence architecture. They do not expose the model. They operate through it — producing outputs that demonstrate coherence without describing it.

04

Ongoing stewardship

Coherence is not a project state. It is a field condition. Ongoing engagement ensures the system continues to generate rather than deplete the energy it needs to sustain itself.

The question is not whether your organisation has coherence gaps.

The question is whether you can afford to leave them unresolved. Every engagement begins with a conversation. Peer-to-peer. No application process.
